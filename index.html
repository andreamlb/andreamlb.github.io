Short Data Story

SF Vehicle Theft Did Not Just Rise. It Moved.

This story follows one argument: San Francisco's vehicle theft pattern changed across years, and the important shift is where and when it happens. Instead of a gallery of unrelated plots, this page is a guided narrative with three visuals that build toward one conclusion.

Dataset and Scope

The analysis uses San Francisco Police Department incident reports from 2003 to 2025. Records are merged from the historical and current datasets, then filtered to category-level views that make long-term patterns comparable.

The focus here is vehicle theft as one case study. The goal is not to explain every category, but to show how a single crime type changes across time, location, and hour of day.

Conclusion

Vehicle theft in San Francisco did not simply move up or down. It shifted across both district and time-of-day. The takeaway is practical: a citywide total is useful for awareness, but targeted interventions require spatial and temporal detail. A better strategy is to match response design to the specific neighborhoods and hours where risk is highest.
